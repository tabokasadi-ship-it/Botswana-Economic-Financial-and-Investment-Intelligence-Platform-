## 📌 About This Platform

The Botswana Economic, Financial & Investment Intelligence Platform is a long-term business intelligence initiative designed to consolidate economic, financial, and investment data into a centralised analytical environment. The platform transforms complex datasets into actionable intelligence for policy makers, financial institutions, investors, businesses, and researchers operating within Botswana.

The platform is being developed in modules, each covering a distinct intelligence domain. Modules are progressively added as development continues.

## 📊 Module 01 — Economic Growth Intelligence

### Overview
An integrated BI platform designed to monitor, analyse, and forecast Botswana's economic performance. The dashboard addresses the absence of a centralised economic intelligence system by consolidating multiple economic datasets into a single analytical environment that supports evidence-based decision making.

### Intelligence Modules
The dashboard consists of 10 interconnected intelligence modules:

| Module | Description |
|---|---|
| GDP Performance Intelligence | Historical growth trends, GDP composition, and sector performance (2015–2025) |
| Economic Health Intelligence | Composite Economic Health Score measuring overall economic condition |
| Economic Shock Intelligence | Analysis of major domestic and global shocks and their impact on growth |
| Diversification & Transformation Intelligence | Non-mining sector contribution and structural transformation tracking |
| Sector Opportunity Intelligence | Sector Opportunity Matrix identifying high-potential growth industries |
| Risk & Opportunity Assessment | Integrated risk evaluation combining growth, sector, and vulnerability data |
| Economic Resilience Intelligence | Economic Resilience Index measuring capacity to absorb and recover from shocks |
| Forecasting & Scenario Analysis | GDP and sector forecasts (2026–2028) with Future Simulator |
| Economic Wellbeing Intelligence | Composite Economic Wellbeing Score combining multiple welfare indicators |
| Early Warning Intelligence | Economic Early Warning System for identifying emerging risks |

### Data Sources
- **Primary:** Statistics Botswana — National Accounts & Sectoral GDP Data
- **Coverage:** 2015–2025

### 📌 Key Findings

The Economic Growth Intelligence Dashboard reveals several important structural and cyclical insights about Botswana's economic performance between 2015 and 2025.

---

**1. ⚠️ Weak and Volatile GDP Growth Pattern**

Botswana's GDP growth is highly volatile, with periods of expansion followed by sharp contractions — particularly during external shocks such as COVID-19 and global demand slowdowns. Recent performance indicates weak or negative growth, reflecting ongoing structural constraints.

---

**2. 💎 Heavy Dependence on the Diamond Sector**

Botswana's economy remains structurally dependent on diamonds, which is the dominant transmission channel for economic performance. The economy is highly sensitive to fluctuations in global diamond demand, prices, and export revenues — making this the primary source of external vulnerability.

---

**3. ⚡ High Exposure to External Economic Shocks**

The shock analysis confirms significant exposure to external shocks including global demand fluctuations, commodity price changes, trade and investment uncertainty, and global financial conditions.

> 🔢 **Shock Vulnerability Index: 71 / 100** — Moderately vulnerable economic structure

---

**4. 🏥 Moderately Resilient but Not Stable Economy**

Botswana has the capacity to recover from shocks, but resilience is not strong enough to fully offset structural vulnerabilities. The economy is recoverable — but not shock-proof.

> 🔢 **Economic Resilience Index: 72 / 100**

---

**5. 🔄 Gradual Economic Diversification is Emerging**

A gradual structural shift away from mining dominance is underway, with growing contributions from finance & insurance, ICT & communication, wholesale & retail, and construction. However, diversification remains incomplete and uneven.

---

**6. 📉 Decline in Mining Share Signals Structural Transition**

Mining and quarrying show a declining contribution to GDP over time. While this reflects reduced dominance, it also signals vulnerability through reduced external earnings and slower global diamond demand. The sector remains strategically important despite its decline.

---

**7. 🎯 Uneven Sector Growth Performance**

The Sector Opportunity Matrix reveals a shifting growth landscape:

| Category | Sectors |
|---|---|
| 🟢 Emerging Opportunity | ICT, Agriculture, Transport |
| 🔵 Strategic Leaders | Finance, Wholesale & Retail |
| 🔴 Weak Performers | Mining-related activities, Utilities |

Growth is becoming more distributed across services rather than extractive industries.

---

**8. 🧠 Economic Health is Moderate but Under Pressure**

Strengths in diversification and resilience are offset by weak GDP growth, high external dependence, and structural mining decline.

> 🔢 **Economic Health Score: 60.8 / 100** — Moderately weak economy under structural pressure
